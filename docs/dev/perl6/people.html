Architecture & Implementation Team

  • Larry Wall is the inventor of Perl, rn, patch, and many other wonderful things. He is the language designer for Perl 6, and has staked out the perl5 to perl6 translator as his own project.
  • Damian Conway is helping Larry Wall with the design of Perl 6, providing a different perspective from most. He also writes the Exegeses, which explain by example Larry's design documents (the Apocalypses).

  • chromatic is the project secretary. He is at least partially responsible for delaying Apocalypse 12 with that Roles idea but lays no particular claim to finding odd bugs. He also writes code.
  • Will Coleda is the parrot project manager, and has worked on a variety of languages targeting parrot.
  • Jerry Gay hacks on perl6 and PGE, and looks after the Parrot test suite.
  • Patrick Michaud is the author of PGE, the parrot/Perl6 Grammar engine. He's also the Perl 6 compiler pumpking.
  • Allison Randal is the current parrot architect and also hacks on the Perl 6 compiler tools. She started the Synopses with Damian. She disavows all knowledge of the buzz around "topic" and "tagmemics".
  • Hugo van der Sanden, knows more about Perl 5 internals than any one person should, which helps to make better decisions for Perl 6.
  • Audrey Tang is the primary force behind Pugs, a perl6 implementation that targets at least four different backends, including JavaScript.
  • Jesse Vincent is the Perl 6 project manager. His job is to herd cats.

Very Helpful People

While not hacking on the design or implementation, these folks provide valuable service nonetheless.
  • Ann Barcomb is the new summarizer: Every week, she'll let us (and lots of other people) know what we've done. Right now she's going through some backlog, but we should be back to realtime in a few weeks.
