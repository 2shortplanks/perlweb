December 6, 2004 - Grant Given to Adam Kennedy for PPI

The Perl Foundation is proud to announce the latest grant aimed at furthering development in Perl, Adam Kennedy will receive $3500 as a development grant to further his work with PPI, a round-trip document parser for Perl source code. PPI parses a contiguous chunk of Perl source as a document and not as code, and is able to perfectly reassemble a parsed Perl document back into the original file (including whitespace and comments).

July 28, 2004 - 2004 White Camel Award Winners

TPF is proud to announce the winners of the 2004 White Camel awards. The award ceremony was held at the O'Reilly Open Source Convention.

Jon Orwant is the original editor and publisher of the Perl Journal. A long-time Perl user, Jon has had a significant impact on the Perl community. Among other things, he ushered in the Perl 6 announcement with the now-famous "coffee mug heard round the world" at The Perl Conference in 2000.

brian d foy founded the Perl Mongers groups at the first Perl conference in 1997, and started the White Camel awards in 1999. He is the publisher of The Perl Review, which just celebrated its first issue in print. He recently returned from year in the desert maintaining Perl modules without electricity or internet access.

Dave Cross is a longstanding member of the Perl community. He started the first non-North American Perl Mongers group, London.pm. He's the current leader of the Perl Mongers groups worldwide, and revived the Perl Mongers group leaders list.

July 20, 2004 - YAPC::NA::2005 Call For Venues

It's time to throw open the call for venues for next year's YAPC::NA.

You can get details on what YAPCs entail at http://www.yapc.org/venue-reqs.txt. When making your proposal, in addition to venue information and dates, do please make sure to include details on airport locations, local public transit (as well as transit to the venue from the airport!), local cultural attractions, and accommodation information. (See the docs for more details) The more information you provide, within reason, the better.

This year we are also making available the different categories, and weight for those categories, for prospective proposers. Please visit http://yapc.org/yapc-crit.txt for more information on this.

Send the proposals to yapc-venues AT yetanother DOT org. Proposals are due by Friday, August 31 2004, with a decision due on September 15 2004. (If the summertime vacation blahs catch everyone out we may extend the deadline. Don't count on that) This gives about 7 weeks, which is short but manageable, and should still fit within most college and university scheduling requirements.

You can watch the YAPC.org home page for any more info or changes."

June 28, 2004 - Call for Venue "YAPC::Europe::2005"

European YAPC's and National Workshops now have a foundation.

The main goal of the newly formed YAPC Europe Foundation is to provide assistance on request to any European crew setting up a conference (be it a YAPC::Europe or a national workshop).

The foundation can bring help in terms of experience from previous organisers, resources: web space, CVS, mailing lists... (online payment will also be offered), etc.

Of course, its chief concern is to make sure there is a yearly YAPC::Europe, and while the Belfast Perl mongers are organizing the YAPC::Europe::2004 conference in Northern Ireland with enthusiasm, YEF is already looking for next years candidates.

Any dedicated crew interested in organizing the YAPC::Europe conference in 2005 should send an informal application, stating their interest, to:

"YAPC::Europe conference proposal" committee@yapceurope.org

Questions about the application and help to present it can also be asked at this same address.

Deadline for applications: August 15th 2004

For general information about the venue requirements, check the YAPC::Venue module on CPAN, and look at the previous venues at:

http://www.yapceurope.org

Norbert Gruener (President)
for YAPC Europe Foundation (YEF)

June 15, 2004 - Grant Given to Jouke Visser for pVoice

The Perl Foundation is proud to announce the latest grant aimed at furthering development in Perl, Jouke Visser will receive $3500 as a travel and development grant to further his work with pVoice, an assistive software framework which allows severely disabled people who can't use a computer's keyboard or mouse to speak. The grant will allow Visser to speak at OSCON, and to author several articles regarding the use of the pVoice framework. For more information on pVoice, please see this perl.com article.
