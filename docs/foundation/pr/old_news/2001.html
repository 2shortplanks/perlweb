December 10, 2001 Perl Foundation Launched

The Perl Foundation operates under the auspices of YAS specifically to promote the use and development of the Perl programming language.

The Perl Foundation is currently seeking sponsors to support its 2002 round of Perl Development Grants, which provide salary and travel costs to enable exceptional Perl developers to devote their time exclusively to improving the language for the benefit of the entire Perl community.

For more information, please visit the Perl Foundation Website.

July 27, 2001 YAS, Perl Monks, Perl Mongers to Merge

Today at the Open Source Software Convention Kevin Lenzo announced a new foundation is being created between these Perl communities. Coming together gives a clear place for support and development of Perl. Not only does this have the blessing of the Perl elders (Larry has agreed to be on the board) but there is corporate interest from the community which recognizes the importance of Perl and free software. YAS will continue the effort for Perl development grants as it has in the past. We will be setting up a vehicle to accept funding, but we still need your active volunteer participation as well. All the details are still being worked on, and you can watch this site for more information as it becomes available.

July 27, 2001 YAS to Join the Unicode Consortium

Thanks to sponsorship from NetThink, Yet Another Society will be joining the Unicode Consortium. Perl will be the first programming language to be represented in the Unicode Consortium. This allows us to stay at the cutting edge of Unicode development. Simon Cozens, Larry Wall and Jarkko Hietaniemi will be the representatives for YAS at the Unicode Consortium.

April 17, 2001 - YAS Supported Perl Help Channel On IRC

Yet Another Society, along with folks from PerlMonks[1] and the Perl Community at large, has started an IRC channel dedicated to helping answer Perl questions. The channel operators, and those there to help, have vast knowledge in all areas of Perl. You can ask questions about anything, Perl related, you wish; CGI, modules, functions, networking, XS, etc... So, please come join us with your questions, and answers! We look forward to seein you.

To connect, use your favorite IRC client and connect to irc.rhizomatic.net, then /join #perl-help to join the channel.

  • [1] http://www.perlmonks.org
