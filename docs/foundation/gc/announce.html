[% page.banner = BLOCK %]Announcing the Grants Committee[% END %] [% page.title = page.banner %] [% page.forcenavigation = 1 %] The Perl Foundation is proud to announce the membership of its Grants Committee.

The function of the Grants Committee is to review applications and allocate available funding to projects which further the advancement of Perl. Recently awarded grants covered the development of Perl, the Perl modules in the Comprehensive Perl Archive Network (CPAN), and Parrot. Future grants will be forthcoming.

The individuals chosen to be committee members represent a wide range of positions in the Perl community, including programmers in charge of the development of Perl.

The two chairs of the committee are Dan Sugalski and Hugo van der Sanden.

The other voting members of the committee are Leon Brocard, Rafael Garcia-Suarez, Nicholas Clark, Brigitte Jellinek and Stas Bekman. Allison Randal (President) is on the committee as board liason, but doesn't have a vote. Committee members can not receive funding.

The Perl Foundation is dedicated to the advancement of the Perl programming language through open discussion, collaboration, design, and code. The Perl Foundation is a non-profit, 501(c)(3) organization based in Holland, Michigan.
